## Main Information / Contents  
### Central Theme   

The book discusses how we can govern artificial intelligences (AI) globally, considering that technology, law, and international institutions are changing. The idea is that the future of AI regulation is not inevitable ‚Äî it is a human choice. (

### Three Facets of Change  

According to Maas, to design an effective global AI governance architecture, one must deal with three structural transformations:  

- **Sociotechnical changes:** how the uses, impacts, and capabilities of AI evolve, altering social, technical, and ethical regulatory conditions.

- **Disruption in governance/international law:** how AI can alter laws, normative instruments, decision-making processes, and international legal institutions.
  
- **Regime complexity:** there are multiple actors, institutions, and norms that overlap or interact, which can lead to both benefits (coordination, innovation) and challenges (fragmentation, norm conflicts, redundancy).

### Trajectory of Global AI Governance so far  

The author maps how global initiatives have evolved in three ‚Äútracks‚Äù:  

- Military AI / AI for military use (since around 2009).

- Narrow AI (conventional uses) since around 2015.

- Advanced or general-purpose AI (base models, systems with broad capabilities) that gained prominence from 2022.

<br>


### Identified Problems  

- The global governance architecture is fragmented. There are many instruments and initiatives, but few are coordinated or strong enough to deal with all types of risks or impacts. 

- There is a risk that these initiatives may not consolidate in time to face the growing challenges of AI, especially in cases of advanced AI. 
- Some existing instruments (laws, international norms, treaties) may not apply well to new AI systems or may be outdated. 

<br>  

### Proposals and Paths Forward  

Maas offers conceptual and practical recommendations to improve global AI governance:  

- Use adaptive regulatory and institutional instruments that can evolve as technology changes. 

- Pay attention to designing robust, coherent, and resilient governance regimes capable of withstanding shocks, fragmentation, and complex interactions. 
- Explore different institutional models: strengthening existing institutions, negotiating new treaties or international agreements, or creating new organizations specific to AI.  

- Evaluate regulatory choices (hard laws versus soft law, unilateral actions, multilateral cooperation), weighing trade-offs between centralization and decentralization, speed versus legitimacy, flexibility versus predictability.

<br>


### Importance and Urgency  


- There is a window of opportunity to act before many impacts of AI consolidate.   

- Decisions made today will have long-term consequences for how societies, laws, and global institutions will deal with profound technological changes.
